ABOUT ME

WHERE I'M FROM

Born on November 28th, 2001 in Kirkland, WA, I have been living in the Greater Seattle Area all of my life. Growing up in Seattle, the tech haven of the Northwest, I developed an entrepreneurial spirit and love for technology at a young age. From hearing my dad talk about different projects he took on at his job at Microsoft to participating in CodeDay hackathons, my experiences led me to kindle a keen interest in the potential applications of computer science. The likes of Jeff Bezos and Bill Gates inspired me to dream of starting my own company.

As a Muslim American from an Indian family, I have also been deeply connected with the Seattle Muslim community. My faith and religious community are a big part of who I am today.

ENTREPRENEUR IN THE MAKING

When I was twelve years old, my friend and I created 3D car models for a racing game called Need for Madness using Java to plot points in a coordinate system. This was my first ever programming experience, and it was fascinating to see how writing simple commands to the computer could translate into something as complex as creating 3-D cars. The “magical” result of clicking a mouse or pressing a key in a game started to make logical sense. From this point on, computer programming became a passion. It was not only a way of expressing my creativity, but the ever-growing impact it has as a field on our society today was so appealing to me. After taking a couple of Java classes and being involved in a few coding projects, I knew that I wanted to major in computer science. After earning an Associate Degree in CS through dual-enrollment (Running Start), I was directly admitted to the UW CSE major. I'm excited to continue my CS education at UW and hope to participate in undergraduate research and internships. I also want to minor in Entrepreneurship to fulfill my dream of creating a successful Startup company.
